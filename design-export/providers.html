What happens after you refer.

1

Day 1 — Acknowledgment

We confirm receipt of the referral within one business day and contact your office if anything is missing.

2

Days 1–3 — Insurance & Authorization

We verify benefits, initiate prior authorization if required, and resolve coverage issues before they reach your desk.

3

Days 2–5 — Medication & Scheduling

We procure the prescribed therapy through our contracted distributors and cold-chain infrastructure. We schedule the first infusion visit at a time that works for the patient.

4

Ongoing — Infusion & Documentation

A CoreFlow RN administers therapy in the patient's home. After each visit, a clinical note is returned to your office — same format, every time.

Clinical standards you can verify.

Credentialed RNs

Care is delivered by credentialed local nursing partners who live and work in your patient's community. Every nurse meets CoreFlow's clinical standards — CRNI-eligible credentialing required — and every visit is supervised under our clinical protocols.

SC Board of Pharmacy

Resident Pharmacy Permit under Reg 99-43(B). Permit #PH-042891.

URAC & ACHC (pursuing)

Actively pursuing URAC Specialty Pharmacy v5.0 and ACHC IRX-NO797, anticipated Q4 2026. Accreditation has been initiated and has not yet been awarded.

Cold chain

Commercially manufactured, pre-compounded medications. USP 659/1079 cold chain. Temperature-monitored storage and delivery.

Documentation

Clinical notes returned to your office after every visit. Made available to payer UM for PA and concurrent review.

Non-compounding

CoreFlow does not perform sterile compounding. All medications are received as commercially manufactured products.

Clinical documentation, simplified.

After each infusion visit, your office receives a clinical note in a consistent, predictable format. This includes:

  • Medication administered, dose, route, and lot number
  • Vital signs (pre- and post-infusion)
  • Adverse event documentation (if applicable)
  • Nursing assessment and clinical observations
  • Next scheduled visit

Notes are returned within 24 hours of each visit. Format is consistent across all patients and visits — designed for easy integration into your EHR.

Conditions and therapies we support.

We administer home infusion therapy for a range of diagnoses. If your patient's condition isn't listed, call us — we'll confirm formulary fit before you refer.

Neuroimmunology & immunodeficiency (IVIG/SCIG)

IVIG and SCIG for CIDP, multifocal motor neuropathy, and other neuroimmune indications, plus CVID, XLA, and hypogammaglobulinemia. We're built for high-volume immunology prescribers who want to move stable patients out of the infusion center and into the home — without losing visibility.

Infectious disease

IV antibiotics, antifungals, and antivirals for conditions including osteomyelitis, cellulitis, endocarditis, and septic arthritis managed post-hospitalization.

Hematology

Iron replacement therapy for iron-deficiency anemia, chemotherapy-related anemia, and IBD-associated anemia.

Rheumatology and immunology

Biologic infusions for appropriate indications per your prescribing authority and plan coverage.

Hydration and supportive care

IV hydration for hyperemesis gravidarum, Crohn's disease, and post-surgical dehydration.

All therapies subject to formulary availability and payer authorization. Call (854) 888-9070 to confirm prior to referral.
