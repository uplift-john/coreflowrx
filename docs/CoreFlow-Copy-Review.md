# CoreFlow Specialty Infusion — Website Copy Review

**Source of truth:** Jason Clapsaddle (CEO) transcript, "Jason <> John connect," Jul 10, 2026
**Copy reviewed:** Current draft, all pages (index, providers, patients, payers, refer, about, careers, contact)
**Prepared by:** Brand copywriter / web strategist

> **How to read this doc:** Section 1 is the CEO's direction in his own words — the authority everything else answers to. Section 2 maps where the current copy diverges from it. Section 3 gives drop-in rewrites. Strategic Notes at the end flag items that are bigger than copy (brand guide, IA, sitemap).

> **Two flags that govern the whole rewrite:**
> - ⚖️ **Accreditation is not yet awarded.** CoreFlow is *pursuing* URAC Specialty Pharmacy v5.0 and ACHC IRX-NO797 (anticipated Q4 2026). Every reference must use "pursuing" and carry the disclaimer: *"Accreditation has been initiated and has not yet been awarded."* The current copy claims accreditation as a present fact in several places. Those are the highest-priority fixes.
> - **MUSC language is not finalized.** Written authorization is confirmed; exact public wording is TBD. Everywhere the copy makes a specific MUSC claim, it should be swapped to the placeholder **`[MUSC_RELATIONSHIP_LANGUAGE]`** and left there until official wording is confirmed. Do not ship any specific MUSC phrasing yet.

## 1. CEO Direction Points

These are the load-bearing statements from Jason. Where the wording is strong enough to use on the site, it's marked **▸ USE VERBATIM**.

**1. Quality is assumed, not argued. Lead with what actually differentiates.**
> "Patients and physicians when they are being referred or making a referral… there is the presumption of quality." … "patients assume that if the doctor is sending them to us that they're going to get high quality care. It's almost like a given." … "it's innocent until proven guilty."

The site should not spend its best real estate *proving* quality. Quality is table stakes. Trust is the assumption the visitor already arrives with.

**2. The three buckets are the whole story. They aren't on the page yet.**
Jason distilled the entire differentiation into three things (John: "thank you for distilling it into those three simple buckets"):
> **(1) Ease of referral** — "what makes us different is ease of referral for the prescriber, ease of creating the order and sending it to us… a streamlined, easy to navigate service initiation process."
> **(2) Speed to service** — "our expectation is within 24 hours of us obtaining insurance approval… we are offering the patient an infusion appointment in their home."
> **(3) Close the loop** — "the last bookend is sending back that nursing note… we close the loop on communication once each infusion is done."

> "If we do those three things… we're going to be better than the other providers in the space."

And critically — this is not yet landing on the page:
> John: "what you just said needs to hit whoever lands on the page right here. **And it doesn't yet.**"

**3. Frame the three buckets on top of assumed trust.** ▸ USE VERBATIM (structure)
> "Here's the three things that you need… to know about us because we're assuming that you trust us and we're assuming that you believe that we can actually deliver the infusion meds at a quality that you can trust. Given those assumptions, here's what you need to know that really makes you want to choose us."

**4. Close-the-loop, in Jason's own voice.** ▸ USE VERBATIM (near)
> "Hey, your patient that you sent to us, Mrs. Jones — she got her infusion yesterday. She did really well, tolerated the medicine well. Since she's got a chronic condition, we've scheduled her follow-up… for the 18th of August." … "we want to close the loop with you by sending you back the nursing note **100% of the time** to let you know that your patient got their infusion, tolerated it well, and here's the follow-up plan."

**5. "Neighbors taking care of neighbors."** ▸ USE VERBATIM
> "we're neighbors taking care of neighbors basically. That's kind of what we're saying there… I think that's an important message."

**6. The local nurse matters more than the pharmacy's HQ.** ▸ USE VERBATIM (near)
> "it matters less that our pharmacy is based in Mount Pleasant. What matters more is that we're — the nurse coming into your home lives in your community." … "you're going to bump into them at the grocery store."

Delivered via **local nursing partners**: "our using local nursing partners who are… based in those communities to deliver that care."

**7. The real contrast is the national, PBM-driven, mail-order model.** (New angle — not in copy)
> "the reality is that so many of these home infusion providers are PBM driven. Like your medicine's getting shipped from Ohio or your medicine's being shipped from Pennsylvania into some other state."

**8. Soften the hard "South Carolina only / one state" lock — make the message more agnostic and local.**
Jason is filing six more state pharmacy license applications in Aug/Sept and worries the SC-only framing could alienate an out-of-state prescriber:
> "let's say I'm a prescriber in Chapel Hill or Durham… Could I see a version of it that doesn't have that South Carolina focus reference on it?"
> "I almost wonder if… we had to morph it… to make it more agnostic… morph that message to the more the neighbors taking care of neighbors kind of thing."

Resolution reached on the call: **don't build geo-dynamic content** (not worth the cost — John: "you're going to spend $200,000… I don't think it's worth it"). Instead **reframe around "local"** — neighbors, community nurses — which is true today *and* survives expansion. John: "what you just suggested is just… using the term local basically." Keep the compelling part; don't genericize it into meaninglessness; don't name specific geographies publicly.

**9. Keep it simple. Don't over-build or over-spend.**
> "you don't have to over-complicate. We can keep the website simple and… not incur any unnecessary expenses."

**10. The goal is raving fans and top-of-mind recall — "bespoke."** ▸ USE VERBATIM (voice)
> "making people raving fans of 'oh my gosh, where has this been all of my life.'"
> "I want to make it… almost bespoke where if a physician needs to refer a patient for home infusion, they think of us first." And a patient who gets care "asks their doctor to be sent to us" next time.

**11. The core target is the neurology / immunology IVIG prescriber — and the play is moving patients out of infusion-center settings into the home.** (Under-surfaced in copy)
> "there's probably nine or 10 [heavy IVIG/Vivgart prescribers] in that group… it's the immunology… branch of neurology."
> Dr. Rosanski "would like to take [150 IVIG patients] out of AIC settings and get home infusion for 150. That's… like 80% of our whole first year's volume assumption with one doctor."
> "if we built our model to meet the needs of that neurology group because they're tough graders… if we can pass the test with them, we can pass the test with most everybody."

**12. This is a pre-launch build. Nothing can claim what isn't awarded yet.**
> "we're legitimately building this thing right now." (Buttons don't work yet; accreditations pending.) Reinforces the ⚖️ accreditation rule above.

## Strategic Notes (beyond copy — for the brand guide, sitemap, and IA)

**A. Reframe the core brand idea from "South Carolina–only" to "local — neighbors taking care of neighbors."** This is the biggest strategic shift in the call. It affects the brand guide's positioning statement, the homepage proof bar, meta/SEO titles ("…South Carolina" is in nearly every `<title>`), and any state-locked language. The reframe must be expansion-safe (six more state licenses are filing in Aug/Sept) *without* naming specific out-of-state geographies publicly. Recommend the brand guide codify "local / community nurse" as a core pillar and retire "one state / 49 others."

**B. Do not build geo-dynamic / location-based content.** Jason floated showing different copy to SC vs. NC visitors; the call concluded it's not worth the cost (~$200K) or complexity. IA decision: keep the site simple and single-version; solve the same problem with "local" language instead. Log this as a closed decision so it doesn't resurface.

**C. Make the three buckets the site's organizing spine, not just homepage copy.** "Easy to refer / Speed to service / Close the loop" should be a repeatable module used on Home, Providers, and (in patient language) Patients. Consider it a named brand framework in the guide. Today the differentiation "doesn't hit the page" — this fixes it structurally, not just cosmetically.

**D. Elevate the neuro/immunology IVIG ICP in IA.** The core buyer is the high-volume IVIG/Vivgart neurology prescriber, and the winning pitch is moving stable patients out of AIC/hospital-outpatient settings into the home (one prescriber ≈ 80% of year-one volume). Consider a dedicated "IVIG & neuroimmunology" or "For infusion-center patients" entry point / landing section, and lead the conditions list with it. This also sharpens the payer cost story.

**E. Introduce the PBM / mail-order contrast as a positioning pillar.** "Not mail-order, not PBM-driven, not shipped in from another state — local nurses and a named team who close the loop." This competitor contrast is currently absent site-wide and is a strong, ownable differentiator.

**F. Pre-launch integrity pass before any public release.** ⚖️ Sweep for (1) accreditation claims — all must read "pursuing / anticipated Q4 2026 / initiated but not yet awarded"; (2) MUSC — all specific claims held at `[MUSC_RELATIONSHIP_LANGUAGE]` until wording is confirmed; (3) fictional testimonials flagged on *every* page (currently flagged on Patients, not Providers); (4) placeholder credentialing data (NPI, NCPDP, permit #, payer names) replaced with real values or withheld. These are launch-blockers.

**G. Voice guardrail for the brand guide.** Jason's own words consistently outperform marketing polish — "neighbors taking care of neighbors," "bump into them at the grocery store," "close the loop," "innocent until proven guilty," "raving fans… where has this been all my life." Recommend the brand guide capture these as approved phrases and set the rule: quality is assumed, differentiation is argued. Keep clinical terminology on prescriber/payer pages (CRNI, URAC, ACHC IRX-NO797, USP 659/1079, cold chain) and plain 8th-grade language with a human phone number on patient pages.
