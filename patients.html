For Patients & Caregivers

Expert infusion care. In your home.

If your doctor has prescribed infusion therapy, you may not need to go to a hospital or clinic to receive it. CoreFlow sends a registered nurse to your home to give you your treatment — at a time that works for you.

Call us: (843) 884-0101 Contact us →

Here's what happens after your doctor refers you.

You don't need to do anything complicated. Here's the process, step by step.

1

Your doctor sends us a referral.

Online, by fax, or by phone. You don't have to set this up yourself.

2

We call you.

Within one to two business days. We introduce ourselves, answer questions, and set up your first appointment.

3

We check your insurance.

We confirm coverage before your first visit and tell you what to expect.

4

A nurse comes to you.

Your CoreFlow nurse arrives at your home with everything needed to safely give you your treatment.

5

Your doctor stays informed.

After each visit, we send a report to your doctor so they always know how you're doing.

That's it. You focus on getting better. We handle the rest.

Nurses who come to you.

Every CoreFlow nurse is credentialed and supervised under our clinical standards.

Our nurses are trained in infusion therapy. Many are working toward or have already earned their Certified Registered Nurse Infusion (CRNI) credential — a specialty certification for nurses who specialize in IV treatment.

When your nurse arrives, they will:

  • Bring all needed supplies and medication
  • Review your health history and any recent changes
  • Administer your treatment safely and carefully
  • Check in with you throughout
  • Answer your questions before they leave
  • Send a report to your doctor

You will meet your nurse before your first infusion. If you have concerns about a specific nurse, let us know.

Questions patients ask us most.

Will my insurance cover home infusion?

Many insurance plans — including commercial plans, Medicare Advantage, and Medicaid managed care — cover home infusion therapy. We verify your benefits before your first visit and tell you what's covered. Call us at (843) 884-0101 with questions.

Do I have to be home alone during the treatment?

No. A caregiver, family member, or friend is welcome to be present. In some cases, having a caregiver at home is required — we'll let you know if that applies to you.

What do I need to have at home?

Our intake team will walk you through this. Most patients need a comfortable place to sit or lie down, a small amount of refrigerator space for medication storage, and access to running water.

How long does each treatment take?

It depends on the type of therapy your doctor prescribed. Some infusions take 30 minutes. Others take a few hours. Your nurse will tell you what to expect.

What if something goes wrong during treatment?

Your nurse is trained to handle infusion-related reactions and complications. If a medical emergency occurs, they will call 911 and stay with you until help arrives. You will never be alone during your treatment.

Can I keep seeing my regular doctor?

Yes. CoreFlow works with your existing doctors. We do not replace your primary care provider or specialist. We report back to them after every visit.

How do I get started?

Ask your doctor to refer you to CoreFlow, or call us at (843) 884-0101 and we can help connect you with your care team.
