About CoreFlow

Infusion care that puts value first.

CoreFlow Specialty Infusion exists because home infusion in South Carolina needed a different model — one that measures success by what prescribers, patients, and payers actually receive, not by what a pharmacy provides.

We are a non-compounding, non-retail specialty infusion pharmacy. We receive commercially manufactured medications, maintain them under pharmaceutical-grade cold chain conditions, and dispatch employed registered nurses to patient homes for administration. That's our model. It's specific by design.

Why we built CoreFlow.

Prescribers referring home infusion patients in South Carolina have historically faced the same set of problems: poor communication from pharmacy partners, inconsistent nursing quality, and referral processes that create more work than they eliminate.

CoreFlow was built to solve those problems specifically — not to be a retail pharmacy, not to be a staffing agency, and not to be a compounding operation. We do one thing: specialty infusion in the home, done well, in South Carolina.

Our structure is intentional. Employed nurses, not contracted. Pre-manufactured medications, not compounded. One state, not a national footprint that treats South Carolina as a market segment.

That approach earned us the trust of MUSC Health — a preferred home infusion partner for MUSC Health. When a major academic health system holds you to their clinical standards, it shapes how you build everything else.

CoreFlow was established as a joint venture with Council Capital, a healthcare-focused private equity firm.
